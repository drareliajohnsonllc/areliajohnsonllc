About Dr. Johnson

A career built at the intersection of scholarship, strategy, and service.

Ph.D.
Sociology & Criminology
Howard University

Dr. Arelia Johnson is a nationally recognized strategist, scholar-practitioner, and public servant whose work sits at the intersection of policy, justice, and lived experience. A proud daughter of Houston's Sunnyside, TX, she has spent over a decade translating the language of liberation into practical tools for institutions, organizations, and movements.

As Harris County's first-ever Chief Officer of Human and Civil Rights — a position she herself created — Dr. Johnson shaped equity infrastructure at the county level, building frameworks that make justice measurable and organizational transformation real. She holds a Ph.D. in Sociology and Criminology from Howard University, where her dissertation research examined how systems fail to name, record, and protect the lives of transgender murder victims.

Her voice moves fluently between the boardroom and the block, between peer-reviewed research and personal testimony. She is the founder of Joy is Strategy™, a convening for women that centers joy as a practical, political, and communal strategy — and the author of Hijacking Equity and the Fight for Justice.

Original Frameworks

Built for practitioners. Grounded in scholarship.

D.J.
Distributive Justice
Equity's Strategic Evolution

Dr. Johnson's original theoretical contribution, positioned as a fourth sociological paradigm. Distributive Justice moves beyond the language of equity toward actionable redistribution of power, access, and resources — grounded in Black feminist, decolonial, and abolitionist intellectual traditions.

Joy
Joy Is Strategy™
Joy Restores. Oppression Depletes.

A framework centered on the radical proposition that joy is not a reward for justice work — it is a tool of it. Joy restores the three capacities that systems of oppression are designed to deplete: imagination, discernment, and solidarity. Delivered as workshops, keynotes, and training intensives.

R.E.D.I.
The R.E.D.I. Framework™
Reimagine · Educate · Define · Innovate

A four-phase organizational transformation model that moves institutions from performative equity commitments to structural change. Used with county governments, nonprofits, and educational institutions to assess, audit, and redesign systems from the inside out.

T.T.M.
Tuesday Transformation Method™
Incremental. Intentional. Irreversible.

A practitioner's methodology for sustaining equity work within hostile or resistant institutional environments. Built on the insight that transformation happens not in grand gestures but in consistent, strategic, small-scale acts of practice and presence.
