About Dr. Johnson

Dr. Arelia Johnson is a nationally recognized strategist, scholar-practitioner, and public servant whose work sits at the intersection of policy, justice, and lived experience. A proud daughter of Houston's Sunnyside, TX, she has spent over a decade translating the language of liberation into practical tools for institutions, organizations, and movements.

As Harris County's first-ever Chief Officer of Human and Civil Rights, Dr. Johnson has shaped equity infrastructure at the county level, building frameworks that make justice measurable and organizational transformation real. She holds a Ph.D. in Sociology and Criminology from Howard University, where her research examined how systems fail to name, record, and protect the lives of transgender murder victims.

Dr. Johnson is also the founder of Joy is Strategy™, a convening for women that centers joy as a practical, political, and communal strategy.

"Joy reconnects us to our humanity by restoring our ability to feel, perceive, and relate beyond mere survival."

What Makes Her Different

Dr. Johnson is more than a social scientist. She is a practitioner. She has built programs and offices, written policy, taught in schools, and curated spaces for community. Her voice moves fluently between the boardroom and the block, between peer-reviewed research and personal testimony. Audiences leave informed and activated.

New Book

Hijacking Equity and the Fight for Justice: A Practical Guide to Language, Liberation, and Leadership in a Time of Fear

Forthcoming  |  Arelia Johnson, Ph.D.

At a moment when the word "equity" has been stripped, weaponized, and legislated away, Dr. Johnson offers something the national conversation desperately needs: clarity. This groundbreaking book dismantles the deliberate confusion around equity language, exposes how power operates through the manipulation of words, and gives readers, whether they are in a boardroom, city hall, or a classroom, the tools to fight back with precision and purpose. Anchored in her original theoretical contribution, Distributive Justice (DJ) and the practical application of Joy is Strategy™, this is a practitioner's guide to language, liberation, and leadership in a time of fear.
